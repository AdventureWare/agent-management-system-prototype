# SaaS Model Components

Use this reference when you need a durable checklist for building or auditing a SaaS financial model.

## Common input blocks

- Customer and growth:
  - leads, signups, trials, demos, win rate
  - free-to-paid or trial-to-paid conversion
  - seat growth, expansion, contraction, churn
- Pricing and revenue:
  - plan prices
  - plan mix
  - usage or overage rates
  - discounts, coupons, annual prepay mix
  - services or setup revenue if relevant
- Cost of service:
  - infrastructure and hosting
  - model or API calls
  - storage and bandwidth
  - email, SMS, auth, search, observability, and other vendor costs
  - support load that scales with customers or usage
- Transaction and channel costs:
  - payment processor fees
  - app-store or marketplace commissions
  - refunds, chargebacks, failed-payment recovery
  - sales commissions when applicable
- Operating costs:
  - payroll and contractor costs
  - tools and software
  - support, success, sales, and marketing spend
  - office, legal, accounting, and insurance where material
- Taxes and compliance:
  - sales tax or VAT assumptions
  - nexus or jurisdiction-specific triggers
  - merchant-of-record or marketplace collection behavior
  - compliance tooling or filing costs where material

## Core output blocks

- MRR, ARR, and net revenue
- gross profit and gross margin
- contribution profit and contribution margin
- CAC, LTV, and CAC payback when acquisition inputs exist
- burn, net burn, and runway
- break-even point
- sensitivity by churn, conversion, ARPU, cost-to-serve, and fee rate

## Modeling hygiene

- Keep one clear time basis across the model.
- Separate inputs, formulas, and outputs.
- Label sourced values with a date.
- Make fixed, variable, and step-fixed costs visibly different.
- Note what is planning guidance versus what requires accounting or tax review.
