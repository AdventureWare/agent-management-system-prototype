# marketing-strategy-architecture

## When to use this skill

- Use this skill when marketing work exists but the strategy behind it is weak, fragmented, or unclear.
- Use this skill when the task needs a coherent answer to who the audience is, what promise should lead, where the funnel is stuck, and which channels deserve attention now.
- Do not use this skill for isolated copy polish, standalone design work, or implementation-only analytics changes.

## Workflow

1. Ground the work in product truth, current business goal, and the smallest credible revenue or activation objective.
2. Name the current funnel bottleneck before proposing tactics. If the bottleneck is unclear, say so and narrow the diagnostic work first.
3. Choose one primary audience and one positioning angle that follows from real capabilities, not wishful messaging.
4. Define the offer framing, channel priorities, and experiment sequence around that audience-bottleneck pair.
5. Attach a lean measurement plan with decision-linked metrics, not a broad dashboard wishlist.

## Project notes

- Prefer one coherent growth thesis over a long list of channels or content ideas.
- Separate verified facts from assumptions and note what would most quickly validate the risky assumptions.
- Push back on tactics that are disconnected from the offer, landing surface, or conversion path.
