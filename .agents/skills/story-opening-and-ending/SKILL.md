# story-opening-and-ending

## When to use this skill

- Use this skill when the main problem is the opening, the ending, or how the beginning and ending relate.
- Use this skill when a story starts too early, lacks immediate pressure, ends weakly, over-explains, or simply stops after the climax.
- Use this skill for both fiction and nonfiction narrative when the user wants stronger first-page pull or stronger final resonance.
- Do not use this skill when the deeper issue is a broken middle that makes the beginning and ending impossible to judge fairly.

## Workflow

1. Determine what promise the opening should make and what meaning the ending should crystallize.
2. Check whether the story begins at the right moment or whether it is spending too long on setup and background.
3. Identify what active pressure, curiosity, or tension is present in the opening sentence, paragraph, and scene.
4. Check what the ending changes: knowledge, relation, emotional condition, significance, or thematic legibility.
5. Remove explanation that comes after the real ending, and remove setup that comes before the real beginning.
6. Re-read opening and ending together so the story feels designed rather than merely started and stopped.

## Project notes

- A strong opening establishes pressure, not just orientation.
- A strong ending usually reorganizes the meaning of what came before rather than restating it.
- Many opening and ending problems are revision problems, not inspiration problems.
- If the opening or ending still feels weak after local edits, the middle or the story engine may be the real cause.
